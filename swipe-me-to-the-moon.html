Swipe Me to the Moon

A Gay Musical

Written and Composed by Agust Smari Bjarkarson (Agust Islandia)
Released July 18, 2025 • 22 Tracks • 62 Minutes

About the Musical

"Swipe Me to the Moon (A Gay Musical)" is a groundbreaking 22-track theatrical work that captures the full arc of modern gay love in the digital age. From the first nervous swipe on a dating app to building a life together and facing life's hardest challenges, this musical tells an unflinchingly honest story about connection, commitment, and the transformative power of love.

Released on July 18, 2025, this 62-minute musical journey follows a gay man's evolution from coming out and navigating the chaotic world of dating apps (Grindr, Tinder, Hinge) to finding genuine connection, moving in together, meeting parents, and ultimately facing terminal illness and loss. It's a celebration of queer love that doesn't shy away from the messy, complicated, beautiful reality of building a life with someone.

The Story

Act I: The Search (Tracks 1-7)

A newly out gay man discovers the overwhelming world of dating apps. From "The Grindr Carol" to endless swiping and ghosting in "What App, Though?", he navigates the frustration and humor of modern gay dating. When he finally gets a match that feels real ("Could This Be It?"), he experiences both hope and heartbreak. But then David appears, and their first date ("Is This Real?") changes everything. The connection is instant, undeniable, and unlike anything either has experienced.

Act II: Building a Life (Tracks 8-17)

The relationship deepens as they navigate intimacy, vulnerability, and commitment. From figuring out sex ("Is There a Guidebook?") to falling completely in love ("I Am in Love!"), they build a relationship rooted in authenticity. They move in together ("Our Place, Finally!"), come out to their parents ("We're Not Just Friends!"), and even take a "Gaycation!" to Mykonos. Through arguments about trust ("The First Argument") and everyday moments of joy ("Every Day Is a Wonder"), they create a life that feels like home.

Act III: Love and Loss (Tracks 18-22)

Their world is shattered when David receives a terminal diagnosis ("What Do We Do With Forever?"). The musical confronts grief, caregiving, and the devastating reality of losing a partner far too soon ("And I Had Him"). Ten years of love, condensed into memories and final moments ("Every Single Day"). The musical concludes with "The Spiral" (in both extended and original versions), a powerful meditation on rising from loss, honoring love, and finding the strength to begin again.

Themes & Messages

  • Modern Gay Dating: The chaos, humor, and heartbreak of dating apps (Grindr, Tinder, Hinge)
  • Digital Connection: Finding real love in a sea of swipes, ghosts, and torsos
  • Coming Out Later in Life: The joy and awkwardness of discovering yourself
  • Authentic Intimacy: Navigating sex, vulnerability, and trust as a gay couple
  • Building Queer Family: Moving in, meeting parents, creating a home together
  • Gay Relationships: The everyday magic of loving someone openly and proudly
  • Terminal Illness: Facing a partner's diagnosis with love and presence
  • Caregiving and Loss: The devastating reality of losing a partner
  • Grief and Hope: Rising from loss and finding the strength to love again
  • Queer Resilience: The power of love to transform, sustain, and endure
