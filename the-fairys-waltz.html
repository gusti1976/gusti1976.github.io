Why This Song Matters

"The Fairy's Waltz" is a historical vignette that transports the audience to pre-Stonewall gay ballrooms in New York's Bowery district. The term "fairy" was a common (often derogatory) slang for gay men and drag performers in the early-to-mid 20th century.

The song celebrates the underground gay culture that thrived despite legal persecution—drag balls, secret kisses, painted faces hiding true identities. It honors the resilience and joy found in these hidden spaces while acknowledging the danger ("Arrest us if you dare").
