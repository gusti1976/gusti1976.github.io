About the Song

"The Ramble" refers to a wooded section of Central Park in New York City that has historically been a famous (and sometimes controversial) cruising spot for gay men seeking anonymous encounters. The song depicts the protagonist sneaking out under false pretenses to engage in public sexual encounters.

The opening spoken exchange—"Where are you going?" answered with a casual lie—immediately establishes the deception at the heart of the song. The Ramble becomes a space of forbidden freedom, where the protagonist feels more alive than anywhere else, at the cost of his relationship.

The closing whisper "If my husband knew about this" crystallizes the double life the protagonist is living—wild liberation in the dark, domesticity waiting at home.
